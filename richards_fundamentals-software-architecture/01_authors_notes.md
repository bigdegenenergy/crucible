# Author Research: Mark Richards & Neal Ford

## Overview

**Fundamentals of Software Architecture: An Engineering Approach** is co-authored by two of the most respected voices in the software architecture community. Both authors are hands-on practitioners with decades of combined experience, making this book uniquely practical rather than purely theoretical.

---

## Mark Richards

### Professional Background

Mark Richards is a software architect with over 40 years in the software industry (since 1983). He is the founder of [DeveloperToArchitect.com](https://developertoarchitect.com), a free resource devoted to helping developers transition into software architecture roles.

His professional focus spans:
- Microservices architectures
- Service-oriented architectures (SOA)
- Distributed systems
- Enterprise integration patterns

### Current Role

Richards works as an independent software architect, consultant, and trainer. He has spoken at hundreds of conferences and user groups worldwide on enterprise architecture topics.

### Why He's Qualified

Richards brings a practitioner's perspective rare in architecture literature. His work on messaging systems (JMS), microservices patterns, and enterprise integration gives him deep technical credibility. His focus on helping developers become architects means he understands the knowledge gaps his readers face.

## Neal Ford

### Professional Background

Neal Ford is a Distinguished Engineer at [ThoughtWorks](https://www.thoughtworks.com/profiles/n/neal-ford), the global IT consultancy known for pioneering agile practices, continuous delivery, and evolutionary architecture. He describes himself as an "Author, Thoughtworker, & Meme Wrangler."

### Education

- B.S. in Computer Science from Georgia State University
- Specialization in languages and compilers
- Minor in mathematics (statistical analysis)

### Speaking & Teaching

Ford is an internationally acclaimed speaker who has:

## The Richards-Ford Partnership

The two authors have collaborated extensively:

1. **Fundamentals of Software Architecture** (2020) — The foundational text
2. **Software Architecture: The Hard Parts** (2021) — Advanced topics on distributed systems
3. **Head First Software Architecture** (2024) — Visual, accessible introduction

This trilogy represents a comprehensive curriculum:
- *Fundamentals* establishes core concepts
- *Hard Parts* tackles advanced distributed challenges
- *Head First* provides an accessible entry point

Their collaboration combines:
- **Richards**: Deep technical expertise in distributed systems, messaging, and enterprise patterns
- **Ford**: Broad industry exposure through ThoughtWorks, plus evolutionary architecture concepts

## Author Perspective & Potential Biases

### Strengths
- **Practitioner focus**: Both authors actively consult and build systems, not just theorize
- **Teaching experience**: Years of training developers shapes their explanations
- **Industry breadth**: Exposure to diverse technology stacks and industries
- **No vendor allegiance**: Neither is tied to a specific platform or tool

### Perspectives to Note
- **Enterprise-oriented**: Their experience skews toward enterprise systems; startup contexts may differ
- **ThoughtWorks influence**: Ford's employer has strong opinions on agile, evolutionary architecture, and continuous delivery that permeate the book
- **Technology agnostic**: While a strength, specific technology guidance is intentionally limited
- **Distributed systems preference**: Both authors have extensive distributed systems experience, which influences their pattern recommendations
