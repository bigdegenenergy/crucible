# Phase 0: The Gatekeeper - Credibility Dossier

## Book Information
- **Title:** Fundamentals of Software Architecture: An Engineering Approach
- **Author:** Mark Richards & Neal Ford

---

## Trust Score

**Score: 9/10**

*Justification:* Both authors are verified practitioners with decades of hands-on experience, extensive publication records with reputable technical publishers (O'Reilly), and no identified conflicts of interest. Their claims are grounded in industry experience across hundreds of client engagements rather than single success stories. Minor deductions for enterprise-heavy bias and ThoughtWorks organizational perspective.

---

## The Origin Story Audit (Survivorship Bias Check)

### Repeatable Process vs. Lottery Win

The book's principles emerge from **repeatable patterns** observed across multiple contexts rather than a single success story:

| Evidence | Assessment |
|:---------|:-----------|
| Richards: 40+ years consulting across industries | Repeatable pattern recognition |
| Ford: 700+ conference talks, 3,000+ presentations | Principles refined through teaching feedback |
| ThoughtWorks: Hundreds of client engagements | Cross-organizational validation |
| O'Reilly Architecture Katas | Community-tested exercises |

Neither author built a single company and wrote about why it succeeded. Instead, they synthesized patterns observed across diverse organizations and technology stacks.

### Silent Evidence Analysis

The book acknowledges limitations:
- Enterprise systems bias (less applicable to embedded, real-time, or ML systems)
- Distributed systems preference (may overcomplicate simple applications)
- Western/tech-industry focus (limited non-tech industry examples)

**Unaddressed Silent Evidence:**
- Failed architectural transformations they consulted on
- Organizations where their advice didn't work
- Survivorship bias in case studies (mostly successful companies like Netflix, Amazon)

### Universal Law Verification

The "Two Laws of Software Architecture" are presented as universal:

| Law | Verification Status |
|:----|:--------------------|
| "Everything is a trade-off" | **Verified** — Consistent with engineering principles, thermodynamics, economics |
| "Why > How" | **Partially Verified** — Sound principle, but "how" matters significantly in implementation |

## Epistemic Foundation Check

### Data Sources (Anecdata vs. Longitudinal)

| Type | Prevalence | Examples |
|:-----|:-----------|:---------|
| **Anecdata** | High | Personal consulting experiences, unnamed clients |
| **Case Studies** | Medium | Netflix, Amazon, Uber (public information) |
| **Academic Research** | Low | Conway's Law, coupling/cohesion metrics |
| **Longitudinal Studies** | None | No multi-year controlled studies |

The book is primarily **practitioner wisdom** codified, not empirical research.

### Citation Quality

| Citation Type | Assessment |
|:--------------|:-----------|
| Academic papers | Rare; mostly foundational CS concepts |
| Industry articles | Frequent; often ThoughtWorks Radar, InfoQ |
| Books | Extensive; canonical texts (Evans, Martin, Fowler) |
| Primary sources | Good for metrics (cyclomatic complexity, coupling) |

### Pseudoscience Markers

| Marker | Present? |
|:-------|:---------|
| Unfalsifiable claims | No — Claims are testable |
| Appeal to authority | Minimal — Relies on reasoning |
| Correlation/causation confusion | Occasional — Some pattern claims lack causal evidence |
| Cherry-picked examples | Some risk — Successful company examples dominate |
| Jargon inflation | Low — Terms are well-defined |

## Red Flags

- ThoughtWorks organizational bias may influence "evolutionary architecture" emphasis
- Enterprise/distributed systems bias may not apply to all contexts
- Star ratings for architecture styles are subjective and context-free
- Limited coverage of emerging patterns (AI/ML, serverless, edge computing)
- Case studies favor successful companies (survivorship bias risk)

**Status:** Verified Expert

Both authors have:
- Verifiable 20+ year track records in software architecture
- Consistent publication history with reputable technical publishers
- Speaking records at major conferences (O'Reilly, QCon, GOTO)
